What's inside the ≈£1.92 spreadthe economics

The spread is not the retailer's profit. UK produce departments typically run a ~31% gross margin (NGA), but fresh fruit loses 13%+ of weight to shrink (USDA ERS, 2016) — soft fruit more — and net food-retail margin is only ~1.7% (FMI, 2024); in peak season fresh produce is often run as a deliberate loss-leader (Richards & Hamilton, 2006). The rest is importer & distributor margin, ripening and freight. These are typical UK produce figures, not a measured split of this fruit's price.

Per-container = ~24 t of fruit per 40-ft reeferhow

Per-container figures take ~24 t of fruit per 40-ft reefer — ~20 standard (1×1.2 m) pallets in a single layer (no double-stacking; airflow gaps). Soft berries and clamshell fruit settle near ~20 t (volume-bound); dense cartoned fruit (apples, citrus, stone fruit) loads ~24 t (weight-bound) — this fruit cartons densely, so it hits the reefer's weight cap (~26 t payload) before it runs out of floor space. 40-ft HC reefer pallet capacity: ICE Transport · FreightAmigo · RFL Cargo.
