The price journey

border to shelf · two measured prices and the spread between them

Landed is the 12-month all-origin average (volume-weighted). Its latest settled month (Apr) ran £7.64. Shelf is a standard 300 g punnet, wk 22 Jun. Note both ends are measured (HMRC border, Trolley shelf), but they sit weeks apart — HMRC lags ~12 wks and fruit reaches the shelf days after landing, so the border price leads the shelf.

A 40-ft reefer (~20 t): £160k at the border → £230k on shelf — a £70k spread to cover everything in between.
What's inside the £3.50 spreadthe economics

The spread is not the retailer's profit. UK produce departments typically run a ~31% gross margin (NGA), but fresh fruit loses 13%+ of weight to shrink (USDA ERS, 2016) — soft fruit more — and net food-retail margin is only ~1.7% (FMI, 2024); in peak season fresh produce is often run as a deliberate loss-leader (Richards & Hamilton, 2006). The rest is importer & distributor margin, ripening and freight. These are typical UK produce figures, not a measured split of this fruit's price.

Per-container = ~20 t of fruit per 40-ft reeferhow

Per-container figures take ~20 t of fruit per 40-ft reefer — ~20 standard (1×1.2 m) pallets in a single layer (no double-stacking; airflow gaps). Soft berries and clamshell fruit settle near ~20 t (volume-bound); dense cartoned fruit (apples, citrus, stone fruit) loads ~24 t (weight-bound) — this fruit is a light clamshell/punnet pack, so it fills the ~20 pallets by volume before it nears the weight cap. 40-ft HC reefer pallet capacity: ICE Transport · FreightAmigo · RFL Cargo.
