The retailer's ~31% slice is gross margin, not profitwhy

That ~31% retail slice is gross, not profit: fresh fruit loses 13%+ by weight to shrink (USDA ERS, 2016) — soft fruit more — so the initial markup is set ~40–50% to net it down; food-retail net margin is only ~1.7% (FMI, 2024), and fresh produce is often run as a deliberate loss-leader in peak season (Richards & Hamilton, 2006). Border & shelf are measured; the split is modelled.

not a journey step UK-grown, British-season wholesale (DEFRA · New Covent Garden) is £13.18/kg (Jun 2026) — premium loose fruit sold spot, a different product/season to imported retail, so it sits above both prices.

Per-container = ~20 t of fruit per 40-ft reeferhow

Per-container figures take ~20 t of fruit per 40-ft reefer — ~20 standard (1×1.2 m) pallets in a single layer (no double-stacking; airflow gaps). Soft berries and clamshell fruit settle near ~20 t (volume-bound); dense cartoned fruit (apples, citrus, stone fruit) loads ~24 t (weight-bound) — this fruit is a light clamshell/punnet pack, so it fills the ~20 pallets by volume before it nears the weight cap. 40-ft HC reefer pallet capacity: ICE Transport · FreightAmigo · RFL Cargo.
